Educational professionals recognize that parents play a critical role in the education of their children (Education for All, 2005; Ontario Curriculum – The Kindergarten Program 2006).
Children would have a physical preparation before they go to school because of the parents’ influence, such as with hygiene, sleep and nutritional needs. Meanwhile, parents can ensure that pupils gain some experience of social interactions, and provide encouragement and praise.

Therefore, there are some helpful methods and activities for parents to lead their children to practice oral language at home. Reading some interesting stories can be a good choice, especially with colorful pictures so that parents would describe the feeling of the characters.
It may make children feel amused and for them to be interested in the story. In addition, children can be encouraged to find and describe the size of objects which are around them by using words, such as small, medium and large.
It is beneficial for parents to lead their children to develop language concepts. On the other hand, parents also can teach children to try to read by themselves.
During this process, when they find some words which they do not know how to read or can not understand, parents would provide suitable help for them so that the learning skill may also develop well.

Moreover, parents can also use the family mealtimes to enhance their children’s ability of an oral language. It may focus on the daily events, make plans for next day, share experiences or answer some puzzling questions.
Children would easily participate and talk with adults. Thus, there are more opportunities for children to hear, know the meanings of words, context, join in using a complex language and gain new information about extended discourse.
Weizman and Snow (2001) claimed that mealtimes were a more richly supportive context for the use of rare words in informative contexts than toy play or even book reading.

Overall, according to the aforementioned, parents play a main role of enhancing children’s oral language skills and if adults can lead their children on the right way in the early childhood, pupils would get a good preparation before they go to school, and it may even have a further impact on children’s lives.

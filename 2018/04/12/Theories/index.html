Theories

Posted on 2018-04-12 | In Theories

For the deep reorganization and development of oral language, some relevant theories need to be explained.

Behavioral Theory

First of all, the behavioral theory mentions that language can be observed and measured. According to B.F.Skinner (1904-1990), who is a well-known behaviorist, children are conditional by their environment to respond to particular stimuli with language. Therefore, when children speak the language of their parents they are rewarded and become more skillful.
They grow in their ability to react in a manner that responds to the environmental stimuli given by their parents. It shapes a child’s language more than the knowledge of rules. (Gleason and Ratner 2009).

The Zone of Proximal Development (ZPD)

Lev Vygotsky, who was a soviet psychologist, claimed that ‘the distance between the actual development level as determined by independent problem solving and the level of potential development as determined through problem solving under adult guidance or in collaboration with more capable peers (happens).
The zone of proximal development (ZPD) defines those functions that have not yet matured but are in the process of maturation.’ (Vygotsky ,1978) It relates to a particular issue that a pupil merely starts to have the ability to solve it so that during this period, indeed, some tasks would beyond the child’s capabilities.

Private & Egocentric Speech

Children’s self-talk, guides their thinking and actions. Eventually, these verbalizations are internalized as silent inner speech which is private speech. (Woolfolk & Margetts, 2016).
Compared with it, egocentric speech represents the children’s self-directed talk. (Piaget,1896-1980). With the growth of children, their intelligence and mental cognition would be enhanced with impacts of their environment.
Therefore, pupils may have their own thoughts which can be different with adults so that sometimes some disagreements may appear.
In general, whether it be private or egocentric speech, both would be helpful for children to regulate their thinking and to be of benefit in developing their language. Because of that, it would encourage pupils to use the ability of self-talk at home or in school.
